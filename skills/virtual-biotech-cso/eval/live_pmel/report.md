## Target overview

PMEL (also SILV/gp100) encodes a melanocyte-lineage glycoprotein expressed in >90% of melanomas and in normal melanocytes, retinal pigment epithelium (RPE), and leptomeningeal melanocytes. It is one of the most clinically validated shared tumor antigens: tebentafusp (ImmTAC gp100×CD3 bispecific, Immunocore) achieved FDA approval in HLA-A*02:01+ uveal melanoma in 2022 with demonstrated OS benefit (Nathan et al. NEJM 2021), and gp100:209-217 peptide vaccine showed OS benefit combined with IL-2 in a 2011 NEJM RCT (Schwartzentruber et al.). The target is most advanced in uveal melanoma; cutaneous melanoma represents an earlier-stage opportunity where gp100-directed approaches are positioned as adjuvant or post-ICI-progression strategies. The dominant modality constraint is HLA-A*02:01 restriction for MHC-I epitope-based approaches (~45% of European-ancestry patients), motivating interest in HLA-agnostic modalities (CAR-T targeting extracellular PMEL domain, multi-epitope mRNA vaccines).

## Liabilities & risks

- **HLA-A*02:01 restriction limits addressable population to ~45% of Europeans and substantially fewer in East Asian (~20%) and African (<10%) ancestry groups, creating a significant equity gap and constraining trial eligibility** — *mitigation:* Multi-epitope strategy incorporating HLA-A*24:02, HLA-B*07:02, and DRB1-restricted PMEL epitopes, or HLA-agnostic modalities (extracellular domain CAR-T, mRNA vaccines covering multiple HLA supertypes); step_06 deferred — quantitative allele frequency analysis required before addressable fraction can be calculated
- **On-target/off-tumor toxicity in PMEL-expressing normal tissues: dermal melanocytes (vitiligo), RPE (ocular AEs including uveitis, potential vision loss), and leptomeningeal melanocytes (theoretical CNS risk) — documented in tebentafusp clinical data but not captured by live tool runs [step_04 returned empty due to incorrect query parameter 'PMEL' instead of 'tebentafusp']** — *mitigation:* Therapeutic index modeling using quantitative GTEx/HPA normal-tissue expression data; dose-titration and ophthalmologic monitoring protocols already established in tebentafusp label; modality-specific AE profiling (bispecific vs. CAR-T vs. vaccine differ in toxicity spectrum)
- **Antigen presentation machinery (APM) loss under ICI pressure — B2M, TAP1/TAP2 loss-of-function in post-ICI melanoma erodes efficacy of all MHC-I-dependent PMEL approaches (tebentafusp, TCR-T, peptide vaccines); step_07 OpenTargets query returned 0 evidence records [step_07], likely due to query parameter failure, not true absence** — *mitigation:* Patient stratification for intact APM (B2M/HLA-A IHC or WES prior to enrollment); parallel development of MHC-I-independent modalities (CAR-T against extracellular PMEL) for APM-loss subpopulation
- **Uveal vs. cutaneous melanoma biological divergence — tebentafusp efficacy data (GNA11/GNAQ-driven uveal melanoma) does not transfer cleanly to BRAF/NRAS-driven cutaneous melanoma; competitive landscape in cutaneous melanoma is dominated by PD-1/CTLA-4 combinations** — *mitigation:* Disease-specific clinical program design with separate trial arms; cutaneous melanoma positioning as post-ICI salvage or combination-ICI adjuvant rather than first-line monotherapy
- **Mature competitive landscape around tebentafusp and IP concentration at Immunocore for HLA-A*02:01-restricted ImmTAC gp100 modality — freedom-to-operate for next-gen variants is uncharacterized [step_08 deferred, step_06_reroute deferred]** — *mitigation:* Next-gen program differentiation via: (a) extracellular domain targeting for HLA-agnostic CAR-T, (b) multi-epitope mRNA vaccine platforms, (c) combination strategies (tebentafusp + checkpoint) not covered by composition-of-matter patents; IP landscape analysis required
