## Target overview

PMEL (Premelanosome Protein, gp100) is a melanocyte differentiation antigen expressed constitutively in >95% of cutaneous and uveal melanomas, normal melanocytes, and retinal pigment epithelium. It is a type-I transmembrane protein processed into amyloid-like fibrils within melanosomes; the cell-surface ectodomain is the structural basis for antibody-based modalities. It has no oncogenic driver role (zero DepMap proliferative dependency; no recurrent somatic mutations) — its therapeutic relevance is entirely immunological. Tebentafusp (IMCgp100), an HLA-A*02:01-restricted TCR×anti-CD3 bispecific, received FDA approval in 2022 for uveal melanoma and represents the first T-cell engager approval in solid tumours. HLA restriction (~45% prevalence in Europeans, lower in other ancestries) and on-target/off-tumour toxicity in normal melanocytes (vitiligo, uveitis) are the primary constraints on current modalities.

## Liabilities & risks

- **On-target/off-tumour toxicity in normal melanocytes (skin, RPE, hair follicle, cochlear stria vascularis)** — *mitigation:* CellMarker confirms 9 normal cell types express PMEL [step_03]; quantitative GTEx/HPA expression profiling of RPE and CNS melanocytes needed to define therapeutic index. Modality choice (ADC vs TCR-T vs vaccine) determines toxicity severity — ADC bystander kill and TCR-T cytokine release differ mechanistically. Ocular monitoring and dose-fractionation mitigated tebentafusp-associated uveitis in clinical practice.
- **HLA-A*02:01 restriction limits addressable population for current leading modality (tebentafusp)** — *mitigation:* HLA-agnostic modalities (ADC targeting ectodomain, extracellular-domain bispecific, mRNA vaccine) could expand eligibility. Structural characterisation of PMEL ectodomain epitope accessibility is the key tractability gate for these approaches [open question: ectodomain topology].
- **Antigen loss and MHC-I downregulation as acquired resistance mechanisms** — *mitigation:* Combination with checkpoint inhibitors or MHC-I restoration strategies. HLA-agnostic modalities (ADC) bypass MHC-I dependency and would remain active in HLA-loss tumours — this is a differentiation argument for ADC development. TCGA HLA-A/B2M somatic loss-of-function frequency in SKCM is an unresolved question [step_01 gap].
- **No oncogenic driver role — PMEL is a lineage antigen with no proliferative dependency** — *mitigation:* This is not disqualifying for immunotherapy: the therapeutic mechanism is T-cell or antibody-mediated killing of antigen-positive cells, not inhibition of a driver pathway. Tebentafusp approval validates this rationale. Frame as TAA, not ODD.
- **Step_04 FAERS query used gene symbol 'PMEL' as drug name, returning empty adverse-event summary** — *mitigation:* Reissue OpenFDA query with drug_name='tebentafusp' and 'IMCgp100'. Known clinical AEs (grade 1–2 vitiligo, uveitis, cytokine release syndrome) are documented in the tebentafusp label and pivotal trial publications but were not retrieved as live data in this run.
